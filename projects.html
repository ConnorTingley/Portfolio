Biped

  • A bipedal robot capable of high-mobility maneuvers in an uncertain environment
  • Uses reaction wheels for persistent control torque on the body and removes the need for ankle joints
    • The wheel's angular momentum storage allows the robot to react to unexpected dynamics such as external forces or sim to real differences in any contact state, including those where no feet are on the ground
  • Controlled by a novel, contact implicit model predictive controller
  • Does not use an explicit contact sequence, instead it optimizes contact along-side path planning
    • The solver chooses the contact sequence that minimizes the cost function, instead of a predefined contact pattern
  • Robot dynamics are paired with a custom, semi-rigid contact model
  • A custom cost function balances path accuracy, reaction wheel saturation, and energy usage
  • Currently uses the Crocoddyl library's FDDP solver, though I am working on a solver that is explicitly designed for changing contact modes
